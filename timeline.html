Solar Cycle 1 (1755 - February 1766)

Solar Maximum: June 1761

  • January 14, 1761 (India): Armies of the Afghan Durrani Empire defeat the Maratha Confederacy and giving power to the Mughal Emperor
  • November 26, 1761 (Mexico): Revolt of Mexico’s Maya population is curtailed by the Army of Spain.
  • December 16, 1761 (Russia): Russians take the Prussian fortress Kolberg. The Seven Years’ War would go on until its conclusion in 1763.

Solar Cycle 2 (1766 - June 1775)

Solar Maximum: September 1769

  • 1768 (Ottoman Empire): Russo-Turkish War Begins with Russian invasion. The war would eventually expand Russian territory and influence.

Solar Cycle 3 (1775 - June 1784)

Solar Maximum: May 1778


  • February 5, 1778 (France): Treaty of Paris is signed, signifying France’s recognition of the United States and causing France to join the American Revolutionary War as an ally of the U.S., escalating conflict with Britain.

Solar Cycle 4 (1784 - September 1798)

Solar Maximum: February 1788


  • May 5, 1789 (France): The Estates General of 1789 is summoned in France after a long period of social, political, and economic distress under poor leadership. This gives rise to the French Revolution after the Third Estate forms its own National Assembly due to disagreements on how general assemblies should be run.

Solar Cycle 5 (1798 - April 1810)

Solar Maximum: February 1805

  • September 11, 1805 (France): Several European countries fought against France and its ally Spain, in the War of the Third Coalition, the first of the Napoleonic Wars.

Solar Cycle 6 (1810 - July 1823)

Solar Maximum: May 1816


  • June 9, 1815 (Austria): The Final Act is signed at the Vienna Congress, altering the European political order after the downfall of Napoleon Bonaparte. A week later, Napoleon’s final defeat at Waterloo brings an end to the Napoleonic Wars.

Solar Cycle 7 (1823 - May 1833)

Solar Maximum: November 1829

  • July 6, 1827 (England): The Treaty of London is signed, recognizing Greece’s independence and increasing Russian grievances with the Ottoman Empire, eventually leading to the Russo-Turkish War of 1828-1829.
  • July 29, 1830 (France): A new government is established in France after the July Revolution, wherein King Charles X is overthrown and replaced with his cousin Louis Philippe.

Solar Cycle 8 (1833 - November 1843)

Solar Maximum: March 1837

  • May 10, 1837 (United States): Banks in New York City run out of gold and silver, resulting in a major financial collapse called the Panic of 1837.

Solar Cycle 9 (1843 - July 1855)

Solar Maximum: February 1848


  • January 12, 1848 (Italy): The Sicilian revolution of independence begins, becoming one of the first of the Revolutions of 1848 in an abrupt and widespread series of revolutions throughout several countries in Europe during the year. This series of revolutions is known as the Springtime of Nations.
  • February 1848 (Germany): German Revolutions of 1848 begin due to discontent with the German political structure.
  • February 21, 1848 (Germany): Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels publish The Communist Manifesto, which went on to become one of the most influential political texts in the world.
  • February 22, 1848 (France): French Revolution of 1848 begins, which would lead to the collapse of the July Monarchy.
  • March 18, 1848 (Sweden): Political uprisings begin in what is now known as the March Unrest
  • March 19, 1848 (Poland): Poland uprising of 1848 begins as Poles lead an unsuccessful military insurrection against the Kingdom of Prussia.

Solar Cycle 10 (1855 - December 1867)

Solar Maximum: February 1860

  • February 8, 1861 (United States): 11 Southern states secede from the United States to form the Confederate States of America with the aim of preserving the institution of slavery.

Solar Cycle 11 (1867 - March 1878)

Solar Maximum: August 1870


  • July 19, 1870 (France): France sends declaration of war to the Prussian government, resulting in the Franco-Prussian War.
  • March 18, 1871 (France): Paris Commune seizes power in France.

Solar Cycle 12 (1878 - December 1890)

Solar Maximum: December 1883

  • August 22, 1884 (China): French Admiral Amédée Courbet is ordered to attack the Chinese fleet, initiating the Battle of Fuzhou and the Sino-French War.

Solar Cycle 13 (1890 - March 1902)

Solar Maximum: January 1894


  • December 1894 (France): Captain Alfred Dreyfus is wrongly accused of treason for allegedly sending the German Embassy French military secrets. He is sentenced to life imprisonment as part of an antisemitic political scandal called the “Dreyfus affair” before being later exonerated.

Solar Cycle 14 (1902 - January 1913)

Solar Maximum: February 1906

  • January 22, 1905 (Russia): Guards outside the Russian tsar’s Winter Palace fire on peaceful workers in what is now known as “Bloody Sunday.” This resulted in widespread demonstrations and strikes against inequality and autocracy of Tsar Nicolas II, also known as the (first) Russian Revolution.

Solar Cycle 15 (1913 - July 1923)

Solar Maximum: August 1917


  • March 8, 1917 (Russia): The (second) Russian Revolution begins in the midst of World War I, eventually leading to abolition of the Russian monarchy and its replacement with the Russian Provisional Government.
  • November 7, 1917 (Russia): Insurrection in Petrograd kick-starts the Bolshevik revolution, resulting in the dissolution of the Russian Provisional Government and rise to power of the Bolsheviks in the beginnings of the Russian Civil War.
  • October 29, 1918 (Germany): Mutiny occurs at Kiel as sailors revolt against military command, bringing about the German Revolution of 1918-1919.

Solar Cycle 16 (1923 - August 1933)

Solar Maximum: April 1928

  • February 11, 1929 (Italy): A key time period in the rise of fascism in Europe. The Lateran Treaty being signed resulted in recognition of Vatican City as independent, allowing Mussolini’s fascist government to gain legitimacy and support from the Catholic church.
  • October 1929 (United States): Stock market crash brings about the Great Depression, a period of economic downturn characterized by severe unemployment and poverty.

Solar Cycle 17 (1933 - September 1944)

Solar Maximum: April 1937


  • July 18, 1936 (Spain): Republican state collapse due to military uprising during the Spanish Coup of July 1936.
  • September 30, 1938 (Germany): Munich Agreement allows for German annexation of part of Czechoslovakia.

Solar Cycle 18 (1944 - February 1954)

Solar Maximum: May 1947

  • January 17, 1947 (Poland): Polish parliamentary elections led to the rise to power of the Democratic Bloc. Under the supervision of the communist Polish Workers Party, opposition was heavily suppressed and punished, and electoral fraud allowed for a pro-communist victory and gave legitimacy to the Polish communist government, however dubious.
  • March 12, 1947 (United States): The Truman Doctrine is announced, pledging America’s support of other countries against authoritarianism. This was the start of the Cold War as the United States worked against the Soviet Union to prevent the spread of communism.

Solar Cycle 19 (1954 - April 1964)

Solar Maximum: March 1958


  • January 1, 1959 (Cuba): Battle of Santa Clara ends as the Cuban city is captured by rebel forces, thus bringing an end to the Cuban Revolution and to the dictatorship of Fulgencio Batista at large.

Solar Cycle 20 (1964 - October 1976)

Solar Maximum: November 1968

  • 1968 (Worldwide): “Protests of 1968” begin as youth worldwide begin to protest social conflicts related to military, civil rights, politics, etc. The most notable causes were protests related to the U.S. involvement in Vietnam, environmentalism, university reform, authoritarianism, and civil rights.
  • January 5, 1968 (Czechoslovakia): Alexander Dubček is elected First Secretary of the Communist Party of Czechoslovakia, giving rise to the “Prague Spring” reform movement.

Solar Cycle 21 (1976 - March 1986)

Solar Maximum: December 1979

  • February 11, 1979 (Iran): Overthrow of the Pahlavi dynasty once the Supreme Military Council is declares an end to the Iranian Revolution, signifying victory for the revolutionaries and a new Islamic Republic of Iran.

Solar Cycle 22 (1986 - September 1996)

Solar Maximum: November 1989

  • June 4, 1989 (China): Student protesters at Tiananmen Square are violently attacked or killed by military troops after months of protesting government corruption and economic unrest.
  • November 9, 1989 (Germany): Berlin Wall falls.
  • December 26, 1991 (Russia): Soviet Union is formally dissolved by vote of the Soviet parliament after Mikhail Gorbachev resigns.

Solar Cycle 23 (1954 - April 1964)

Solar Maximum: November 2001


  • September 11, 2001 (United States): Four planes are hijacked by al-Qaeda terrorists in the deadliest terrorist attack in history, with two crashing into the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center, one crashing into the Pentagon, and the other crashing into a field in Pennsylvania. Attacks were motivated by United States involvement in the Middle East.

Solar Cycle 24 (2008 - December 2019)

Solar Maximum: April 2014

  • June 30, 2013 (Egypt): Protests begin in Egypt motivated by increasing authoritarianism and religious political agenda in what has been reported as one of the biggest protests in Egyptian history. The protests resulted in the 2013 Egyptian coup d'état.
  • February 18, 2014 (Ukraine): Deadly protests break out in Independence Square in Kyiv as rioters demanded the restoration of the 2004 Constitution. Protests led to the 2004 Constitution being restored and President Yanukovych being overthrown.

Solar Cycle 25 (2019 - December 2030)

Solar Maximum: October 2024


  • October 7, 2023 (Israel): Gaza war begins after Hamas launches a terrorist attack into the Gaza envelope of southern Israel in response to Israeli occupation and blockade of the Gaza Strip.
  • July 1, 2024 (Bangladesh): The July Revolution begins as students lead protests related to quota reform and human rights abuses. Demonstrations resulted in government-led mass killings of protesters and eventually led to the resignation of the Prime Minister.
  • December 8, 2024 (Syria): Fall of the Assad regime occurs after the over-a-decade-long Syrian Revolution, motivated by human rights abuses and political and economic unrest.
