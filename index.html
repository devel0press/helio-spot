The solar cycle, which refers to the cycle of changes in the activity of the Sun indicated by the appearance of sunspots, lasts approximately eleven years and is suspected to be correlated with major societal events and civil unrest. Since the 19th century, scientific evidence has supported the hypothesis that the behavior of both humans and animals is in part correlated with environmental factors such as solar and geomagnetic activity, with military conflict being the most evident example. Curiously, scientists have discovered a strange pattern in which wars and other indicators of social and economic unrest are more likely to start at maximum points of the solar cycle (known as “solar maximums”).
